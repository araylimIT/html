Introduction

Abai Qunanbaiuly

Abai Qunanbaiuly (1845-1904). Abay was born in 1845 at the bottom of the Chingiz Mountain in the today's Abai district (former Karkaraly) located in Eastern Kazakhstan region (formerly, the Semipalatinsk region). He was a well-known Kazakh poet, a great thinker, composer, philosopher, the founder of written Kazakh literature, and its first classic.

Childhood

Abai received his primary education at the aul(village) school, later, when he turned 10, his father sent him to the Semipalatinsk Madrasah named after Akhmet Riza. Little Abai was sharply different from his peers inquisitiveness and inquisitive mind. He read the works of Navoi, Nizami, Firdousi. At the same time, he is studying at a Russian school and by the end of his five-year studies he begins to write poetry. However, his father wanted his son to learn how to rule people, and did not allow him to finish his studies. From the age of 13, participating with his father in the management of the people, Abai gathered talented young people around him. In 1883, a public library was opened in Semipalatinsk, and Abai became its regular reader.
